More about Thalassemia

  • Thalassemia is an inherited blood disorder, manifests when the child inherits defective gene from both the parents ( who are the carriers i.e. carry single defective gene along with a normal gene, so they donot manifest ).
  • Body of the sufferer makes less or abnormal hemoglobin leading to anemia, therefore requires blood transfusions for survival. The patient need to undergo blood transfusion every fortnight.
  • There are three types of thalassemia : Thalassemia Major needs blood transfusion within 6 months of age. Thalassemia minor donot need blood transfusion and mostly remain asymptomatic. Thalassemia intermedia may be transfusion dependent or independent. Usually they require blood transfusion within 2 years of life or sudden fall of hemoglobin during fever, acute infection.
  • However with due course of time, they develop iron overload which brings up many complication like growth retardation, delayed puberty, osteoporosis, diabetes and hypothyroidism, venous leg ulcers etc..
  • Life long these patients need either blood , iron chelators and multispeciality approach to manage the complications. Treatment : It is divided in three categories : 1) Transfusion regimen : According to the national guidelines of hemoglobinopathies management and prevention 2) Pre-transfusion regimen : Iron chelators : Regular monitoring of SERUM FERRITIN, liver function test at least once in 3 months. Chelators may be used either in monotherapy or dual as per the recommendation of physician. Management of complications : Under construction...
